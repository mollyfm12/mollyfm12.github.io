Shark Finning

Shark finning is the practice of catching sharks, removing their fins, and discarding the rest of their bodies, often while the shark is still alive. The fins are primarily used in shark fin soup, a popular, luxury dish in Southeast Asia. This practice is very harmful to the ecosystem. Efforts to ban shark finning have increased in order to maintain balance in the oceans.

There are many different species of sharks. See below for more information.

Species Habitat Diet
Great White Shark Costal waters: north coast of North America, Austrailia, South America mammals: seals, sea lions
Hammerhead Shark Warm costal waters: near coral reefs fish, squids, rays
Whale Shark Tropical warm oceans plankton, small fish, krill
Tiger Shark Coastal tropical/subtropical habitats fish, squid, sea turtles, birds
Bull Shark Salt and Freshwater habitats fish, dolphins
